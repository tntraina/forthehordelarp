OK! So we've got a group ready for formulate a bid for the World Media Fandom Convention! Now we need to set up our official committee structure! We have three options we can use:

  • Large, Flat, Open Committee: This lets anyone who might be able to make small contributions to the bid be part of the committee. But in practice, a small number of people will be doing 90% of the work, and most people won't respond to requests to do anything to help.
  • Small, Centralized Committee: This is a more traditional approach where a small number of people are selected to be the core committee members. They are responsible for making decisions and coordinating the bid.
  • "Upper" and "Lower" Committee: Either the best or the worst of both worlds: It includes:
    • a small number of high-level members (the "upper" or "executive" committee)
    • a larger number of lower-level members (the "lower" or "general committee")
    The upper members make decisions, and while the lower members ratify some of those decisions, this can often lead a large number of lower committee members to resign over seemingly trivial decisions they felt the Executive Committee railroaded through the general committee.

You have no personal horse in this race, but need to explain the benefits and drawbacks of each approach to the group, and then facilitate a discussion to help them come to a consensus on which approach to take.
