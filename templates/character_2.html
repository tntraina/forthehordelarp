Galaxy Brain Games is a large gaming company that hosts a series of urban fantasy & horror larps. They want to premiere their new line of sourcebooks, Jiangshi: The Chipper, with an inaugural larp at WMC. Since the materials haven't been published yet, there's no way of knowing what sort of cotnent the game will have. Except that apparently it involves Jiangshis (chinese hopping vampires). Being the world premiere for such a game could be a big draw for attendees and generate significant buzz, assuming the game is well-received. But GBG has a history of producing popular games. Vampyr: The Mopey and Lycan: The Mangy are two of the most popular games of all time, and they have a large and dedicated fanbase. So there's a pretty good chance that the new game will be well-received, and hosting the premiere at WMC could be a big win for the con.

{% endif %} {% if 26003 in game_events %}

Your Asian attendees are livid at the idea of a larp based on a Chinese folklore monster. They are demanding that the con not host the event.

Some parents are concerned that the larp will be too scary for their children. They are demanding that the con not host the event, or at a minimum that it be held after hours and have door staff there to keep children away from it.

It turns out Galaxy Brain Games' new game generated a lot of buzz on its own! People love the idea of a game based on non-European folklore, and one of the main designers is herself a Chinese game designer with a Masters in folklore. It seems like a low-level staffer leaked some details about WMC's refusal to host the premiere, which generated a fair bit of negative press for the con.

{% endif %} {% if 36007 in game_events %}

Word gets out that WMC had the chance to host the premiere of Galaxy Brain Games' new game, but refused to do so because of the not-safe-for-children content. The con gets accused of catering to religious and social conservatives, and some die-hard GBG fans try to get an online petition to boycott WMC going. It doesn't meaningfully reduce attendance count. But it does generate some of negative press for the con, and it gains a reputation for being a more conservative con than some of its competitors.

{% endif %} {% if 36005 in game_events %}

You run the game anyway. There's some minor controversy, but the game is well-received and generates a lot of buzz for the con. The larp doesn't end up being as scary as some parents feared, and the door staff do a good job of keeping children away from it. It turns out one of the main designers of the game is a Chinese game designer with a Masters in folklore, and the game is praised for its respectful and accurate portrayal of its subject matter. Attendance is above expectations, and the con is a big success.
